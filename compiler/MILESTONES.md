# Self-hosting milestones: the road from V0.2.x to V0.3.0

Completing the self-hosted compiler is the V0.3.0 release. The V0.2.x line proceeds
toward it, each release landing a stage increment or a prerequisite. This document is
the release-by-release plan and the migration strategy. The architecture design is in
[`docs/roadmap/V0_3_0_SELF_HOSTING.md`](../docs/roadmap/V0_3_0_SELF_HOSTING.md), which
stays authoritative for the shape of the compiler. The migration strategy below is an
operator decision that revises the earlier forward, byte-identical migration ordering
described there. Version numbers past V0.2.2 are a plan, not a promise; the strategy is
the load-bearing part.

## Migration strategy

This section is the Keleusma instantiation of a general, project-agnostic method,
[Incremental Self-Hosting by Backward Migration](../docs/reference/INCREMENTAL_SELF_HOSTING.md),
which states the same strategy without Keleusma nouns for any language author to reuse.
The subsections below apply it here.

The self-hosted compiler has three streaming stages, lexer then parser then codegen,
and it is ported from the last stage to the first. Codegen goes first, then the parser,
then the lexer. The reason is risk. The emit and wire boundary is the wall that stalls
self-hosting efforts, so it is proven first, and the lexer, which is the easy part,
comes last. This reverses the convenience-first ordering the design document originally
described.

### The moving adapter seam

At any moment exactly one throwaway adapter sits at the frontier between the Rust
upstream and the Keleusma downstream. The Rust stages above the frontier run as a batch,
and the adapter maps their batch output into the nominal stream that the first Keleusma
stage consumes. The Keleusma stages below the frontier are already written and chain
directly to one another. Each time an earlier stage is ported, it supersedes the adapter
that stood in for it, and a new adapter appears one position upstream.

### Adapters are throwaway prototypes

An adapter's output is real, permanent work. It is the Keleusma inter-stage data shape
that the pipeline will carry forever. An adapter's implementation, the Rust to Keleusma
conversion and the batch to stream mapping, is a throwaway prototype of the output
behavior that the not-yet-written upstream Keleusma stage will eventually produce.
Writing that upstream stage is what retires the adapter.

Adapters need to be good enough to port the chain, not perfect, not general, and not
efficient. There is a real tension between fixing an adapter and writing the upstream
stage that will replace it. When writing the upstream stage is the higher-value move,
take it. The goal is stages, not adapters.

### The correctness gate and the deferral ledger

rkyv is not a constraint. It was a means to serialize bytecode, and the self-hosted
compiler does not need to reproduce its byte layout. Serialization is delegated to a
host emit native, or replaced outright when that serves the end better.

The primary equivalence check is structural equality of the logical module, comparing
the module the Keleusma chain produces against the module the Rust compiler produces at
the level of chunks, opcodes, layouts, and the auxiliary tables, rather than at the
level of serialized bytes. Behavioral equivalence over the corpus, compiling both and
running and comparing observable yields and results, is the secondary check.

Correctness is a judgment call backed by a ledger. Each corpus program is either
processed correctly by the current chain, or its deviation is recorded in a deferral
ledger that names the upstream stage which will correct it. When that stage lands, the
deferred cases are re-run and confirmed resolved. A deviation that its responsible
upstream stage does not fix was never an adapter limitation, and it is a real bug in the
Keleusma stage that produced it. Without the ledger and the recheck, the judgment call
becomes a place for stage bugs to hide.

### Two engineering modes

During migration, engineering defers. It prefers building the next stage over
perfecting a throwaway adapter, and it records what it defers in the ledger. Once all
three self-hosted stages exist and the adapters are gone, engineering switches from
deferred work to bug fixing. The deferral ledger is driven to empty, and the
all-Keleusma chain must process the corpus correctly with no adapter left to defer to.
An empty ledger and a passing corpus under the all-Keleusma chain is the V0.3.0
acceptance gate. Every intermediate deferral is a debt against it.

### Stage decomposition

Each of the three stages is many increments. The Rust compiler was not written in three
iterations, and the translation will not be either. Codegen is the largest, because in
the streaming design it fuses typecheck, monomorphization, compile, and emit for one
declaration at a time. It is migrated inside-out, mirroring the same backward discipline
one level down. The codegen input adapter first delivers already-typechecked and
monomorphized declarations, so the first increment is the emit-to-host native plus a
logical-module round-trip, then compile, then monomorphization, then typecheck, with the
adapter delivering progressively-less-processed declarations at each increment.

| 1 | **Codegen** (`kel/codegen.kel`) | The largest stage, migrated inside-out over several increments: the emit-to-host boundary plus a logical-module round-trip first, then compile, then monomorphization, then per-declaration typecheck. Increment 0 proved the emit boundary; increment 1 the op vocabulary; increment 2 the first real codegen from input (single-node bodies). Increment 3 is the **recursion-free work-stack tree walk** (R3.1), the design's biggest tension after the emit boundary: the stage drains an explicit work-stack, one work item per `loop` iteration, emitting a post-order op stream for a general binary-arithmetic body of literals, parameters, and `+`/`*`, including the checked-arithmetic-then-`PopN` lowering, reaching `input * 2 + 1` computed from its tree rather than transcribed. Increment 4 has the stage **own its constant pool**: literals now carry their value, the stage interns each into a pool as it walks, emits `Const` at its own index, and emits the pool after `Return`; the host builds the module from the stage's ops and pool rather than the reference's, removing the adapter's index-precomputation crutch. Increment 5 **deduplicates the pool**, mirroring the reference compiler's constant interning (`compiler.rs add_const_value`): before appending a literal the stage scans the pool with a bounded `for` over its fixed capacity and reuses a matching index, so a repeated literal produces one entry and aligned indices. The scan accumulates the match index in a `data` field, since locals are immutable. Increment 6 compiles a **block of `let` bindings** followed by the tail expression: each `let x = e;` lowers to e's ops then `SetLocal(slot)`, slots assigned after the parameters in declaration order, and identifier references resolve to a parameter or `let` slot; the stage seeds its work-stack with the tail deepest and each statement's (VISIT-expr, EMIT-SetLocal) pair pushed last-to-first so the LIFO drain emits statements in source order, then the tail, then `Return`. Increment 7 lowers the **full binary integer arithmetic set** (`+ - * / %`): a per-operator lowering in the BinOp branch selects the op word and whether the result needs the two-word `PopN(2)` fixup, so the three checked operators (`+`/`*`/`-`) take the `PopN` and `/`/`%` (single-word `Div`/`Mod`) take none, matching the reference lowering. Increment 8 adds the **six comparison operators** (`==`, `!=`, `<`, `>`, `<=`, `>=`), each a single `Cmp*` op with no `PopN` and a `bool` result; crossing sixteen op tags exceeded the old `tag + operand*16` op-word radix (tag 16 would alias the `PENDING` marker), so the op-word radix widened to 32, a self-contained protocol change between the stage and its host driver with no runtime `BYTECODE_VERSION` impact. Increment 9 adds **`if`/`else` structured control flow** (expression form): an If node lowers to the condition ops, an `If` marker, the then-branch ops, an `Else` marker, the else-branch ops, then `EndIf`, matching the reference's structured lowering, and validated on nested `if` as well. The `If`/`Else` operands are absolute forward op indices the reference bakes in, but a single post-order walk cannot know the forward branch sizes when it emits the markers, so this increment emits placeholder targets and the host assembler resolves them by bracket-matching the nested markers. This mirrors how the constant pool first arrived with host-supplied indices (increment 3) before the stage owned it (increment 4). Increment 10 **pulls target resolution into the stage**: rather than stream one op per yield, the stage appends op words to a bounded `ops` buffer in the `data` segment and, as it appends an `Else`/`EndIf`, backpatches the matching `If`/`Else` target in place using a control-flow position stack, then streams the resolved buffer; the host `resolve_targets` step is gone and the emitted stream carries the reference's absolute targets directly. Backpatching a fixed-size buffer keeps memory statically bounded. Increment 11 has the stage **emit its own `local_count`** (the local-frame size, `param_count + let count`), streamed as one raw Word after the pool; the host builds the chunk with it and asserts it agrees with the reference. This is the one chunk-header field codegen computes; the remaining header fields (name, parameter arity and types, block type) are declaration data the parser and type checker produce, not codegen products, and stay input-adapter transcription. So the stage now emits every field codegen owns for this grammar subset. Increment 12 makes **blocks first-class and nestable**: earlier increments carried a block's `let` bindings in a top-level statement array, so `let` could appear only at the body's top level; this increment folds blocks into the node forest as a `LetIn` cons-list (`let x = e; rest` -> node `LetIn(slot, value, continuation)`), so the top-level body and each `if`-branch are the same thing (a block root), and `let` nests inside branches. Slots are assigned in tree-walk order and never reused across branches (matching the reference), so `local_count` is the parameters plus the number of `LetIn` nodes the walk processes, which the stage counts directly. Increment 13 adds **unary `not`**, which lowers to the operand ops followed by a single `Not` op. Increment 14 adds **function calls**: the argument ops are emitted left-to-right, then `Call(chunk_index, arg_count)` (the two operands packed into the op word's operand field); the callee chunk index is resolved-reference data the adapter supplies by name (name resolution is an upstream concern, not codegen arithmetic). Increment 15 adds the **short-circuit booleans `andalso`/`orelse`**, which reuse the structured control flow (`Dup` then `If`/`PopN(1)`/`Else`/`EndIf`, with a `Not` inserted for `orelse`); the existing bracket-matching backpatcher happens to produce exactly the reference's targets, and no temporary slot is allocated. Increment 16 is a **pure refactor**: the per-node scheduling, which had grown into a nine-deep `if`/`else` chain, is factored into one `fn` per node kind that mutates the work-stack `data`, and the dispatch is flattened to a flat sequence of `if nk == K { push_kind(payload); }` statements; the emitted op stream, pool, and `local_count` are byte-identical, which the unchanged conformance suite confirms. It also establishes that a Keleusma `fn` may mutate the `data` segment and that such a factoring still verifies. Increment 17 rounds out the operator surface with unary negation (`CheckedNeg` + `PopN(2)`) and the per-limb bitwise operators `band`/`bor`/`bxor` (single-op, no `PopN`); each was a one-line lowering-table and dispatch addition, a payoff of the increment-16 refactor. Increment 18 is another **pure refactor**: the two remaining nested `if`/`else` value dispatches (the node dispatch in the `loop` body and the operator-word mapping in `push_binop`) become flat `match` statements. multi-clause function heads (which the surface does support) were considered but a local `match` reads better for a value-to-value mapping. Increment 19 then **flattens the whole `loop` body into a phase dispatch**: the seed, walk, and five drain phases, previously nested five deep, each move into a `fn`, and `main_phase` (a guarded `match` — guards do work inside a `match`, unlike on a function head) selects the phase, so the body is one flat `match main_phase() { k => yield step_k(), ... }`. The `yield` stays in the Stream block, as the verifier requires (a `loop` that only delegates to a yielding `fn` fails verification), while the per-phase work lives in the steps. Increment 20 then replaces the `match main_phase()` indirection with a **multiheaded `loop`**: Keleusma supports multiheaded functions with `when` guards in all three categories including `loop` (the guard follows the return type), so the state machine is one `loop main` head per phase, guarded on the `data` state and tried top to bottom; each head yields directly. Two notes on the language, corrected from earlier increments. First, function-head guards *are* supported (the `when` follows the return type, `-> Word when cond`); an earlier increment wrongly concluded otherwise from a syntax error. Second, a `loop` that delegates its only `yield` to a called `fn` is still rejected, but this is a conservative-verification rejection, not a fundamental one: the productivity analysis (`verify.rs analyze_yield_coverage`) is intra-procedural and does not follow `Op::Call` into a callee that provably yields on all paths; a multiheaded loop sidesteps it because each head yields directly. Increment 21 acts on both notes. The runtime enhancements (the category-call discipline enforced in `src/typecheck.rs`, and productivity-by-delegation in `src/verify.rs`, both landed as parent-crate changes) make delegation admissible, so `loop main` is now just `emit_next(resume)`, delegating its single yield to a multiheaded `yield emit_next` phase machine that yields on every path; the self-hosted compiler thus exercises the new productivity analysis end to end. Increment 22 adopts the pipeline idiom where it reads well: the node dispatch in `walk_step` pipes the kind field into a `match` (`ast.kinds[payload] |> match { ... }`), exercising the parent crate's newly-completed `|>` support (the `_` placeholder honored by the type checker, and `|> match` as a pipeline target). Increment 23 opens the **data-segment frontier** with scalar reads and writes: a `d.field` read lowers to `GetData(slot)`, and a `d.field = e` statement lowers to the value ops then `SetData(slot)`, generalizing the block cons-list from `let`-only to a sequence of `let` and data-assignment statements; the data slot is the field's index in the module data layout, resolved by name by the input adapter (declaration data, like the parameter arity). Increment 24 adds **array indexed access**: reading `d.arr[i]` lowers to the index ops then `GetDataIndexed(base, len)`, and `d.arr[i] = v` to the value ops, the index ops, then `SetDataIndexed(base, len)`, where `base` is the element-0 data slot and `len` the array length, both resolved by name and packed as base + len*65536; a decomposed IndexStore node sequences the value and index so no node needs three children. Single-dimension only. Increment 25 adds range **`for` loops**: `for i in start..limit { body }` lowers to the loop-variable and limit initialization, a `Loop`/`BreakIf`/`EndLoop` frame whose targets `emit_op` backpatches with a control-flow loop stack (the `Loop` and `BreakIf` exit past the `EndLoop`, the `EndLoop` back-edges to the condition), the body (a statement block ending in a `Unit`, `PushImmediate(0)`, then `PopN(1)`), and a synthetic `i >= limit` condition and `i + 1` increment; the loop variable and limit are two locals, so `local_count` grows by two. The seven per-loop pieces ride in a `for_parts` side array, and the op-word radix widened from 32 to 64 to fit the new tags. Crossing this feature confirmed a thesis boundary: a runtime bound (`0..n`) compiles but fails the strict worst-case-execution-time verifier at `Vm::new` because the iteration count is not statically extractable, so only statically-bounded ranges are runnable. Increment 26 adds `const data` field reads, which inline to the field's literal value exactly as the reference does (no runtime slot), so a `for` loop bounded by a `const data` field (`0..cd.n`) is statically bounded and runs, unlike a parameter bound. It is an adapter-only change: the stage already emits a `Const` for a Literal node. Increment 27 adds **statement-form expressions**: a bare call statement or a statement-form `if` (both `Stmt::Expr`) becomes an ExprStmtIn node that emits the expression ops then `PopN(1)` to discard the value; an `if` without `else` synthesizes a Unit else so both branches produce `PushImmediate(0)`. This closed the two most common self-compile-probe gaps and lifted coverage of codegen.kel's own atomic functions from 10 to 24 of 27; the remainder are `match` (two functions), the non-atomic `yield`/`loop` functions, and two functions that exceed the adapter's 64-node arrays. Multi-dimensional arrays and array-iteration `for` loops are later increments. All are validated by structural equality against the Rust compiler and by running the built module. Increment 28 adds **`match` over an integer scrutinee** (literal arms plus a wildcard), lowered to the reference's virtual-loop dispatch (`GetLocal`/`Const`/`CmpEq`/`If` per arm, an unconditional `Break`, and a dead `Trap` tail), reusing existing ISA ops through new stage-private op-word tags and a bare-`If`/multi-`Break` backpatch. Increment 29 grows the flat-node arrays and buffers from 64/256 to 512 so the stage's own largest functions (`emit_op` at 308 nodes) fit, and every atomic function then self-compiles. Increment 30 teaches the stage the **bounded-runtime-range `for .. limit` loop** (a compile-time counter gives the verifier the bound while the runtime range is tested alongside, with the count-equals-cap boundary reclassified to completion and an overrun trapping), then rewrites the stage's own `for i in 0..64 { if i < n }` scan loops to `for i in 0..n limit 64`. Increment 31 emits the **function-category wrapper**: a `loop` opens with `Stream` and closes with `PopN(1); Reset` instead of `Return`, so `loop main` self-compiles. Increment 32 lowers **multiheaded `yield` dispatch and `yield` expressions**: each head copies its parameters, tests its `when` guard with a bare `If`/`EndIf`, runs its body and returns (the final unguarded head returns directly), closed by a dead `Trap(NoMatchingHead)`, and a `yield <expr>` is the expression ops then `Yield`. With increment 32 the multiheaded `yield emit_next` self-compiles, so **all 34 of codegen.kel's functions round-trip through the stage byte for byte**. The self-compile probe groups heads by name and reads a multihead's op stream to its terminating trap.

Deferral ledger. No host codegen crutch remains for the current grammar; the stage produces the ops, the constant pool, the resolved jump targets, and the local-frame size. | 2 | **Parser** (`kel/parse.kel`, the merged stage; the pre-merge `kel/parser.kel` and `kel/body.kel` are retired) | Recursive descent over the grammar using the work-stack discipline (R3.1), yielding one `Declaration` per top-level declaration into the finished Keleusma codegen. **Increment 1 landed** (`tests/selfhost_parser.rs`): a token-consuming `loop` that recognises each top-level `fn` declaration's kind and name and yields one declaration word (`dkind + name*16`) per declaration, brace-matching the parameters, return type, and body rather than parsing them, validated against the reference parse's function sequence. This establishes the token-input contract, the per-declaration yield discipline, and boundary/kind recognition. **Increment 2 reads the declaration header**: the function category (from the `fn`/`yield`/`loop` keyword, encoded as dkind 1/2/3) and the value-parameter count (the number of `:` colons inside the parameter parentheses; the count now rides at `dkind + pcount*16 + name*1024`). The parameter count correctly excludes const-generic and trait-bound colons, which sit before the parentheses at parenthesis depth zero, and Keleusma types carry no `:`, so a tuple-typed parameter counts once. Each head of a multiheaded function is a separate declaration, so the parser yields one header per head. **Increment 3 moves to a record-per-declaration stream and extracts the parameter names**: a `START` element opens a declaration with its category and name, one `PARAM` element follows per value parameter carrying its name, and an `END` element closes it (`dkind + val*16`, dkind 1/2/3 START, 4 PARAM, 5 END, 15 DONE). A parameter name is the first identifier of each value parameter; extraction is armed only inside the first parenthesis group and disarmed once it closes, so type parameters (before the parentheses) and a return-type tuple (its own parentheses after the parameter list) are not mistaken for parameters. The record model is the shape the body-node stream of later increments reuses. **Increment 4 completes the signature**: each value parameter now also carries a `PTYPE` element with its type name and the declaration a `RETTYPE` element with the return type name (dkind 6 PTYPE, 7 RETTYPE), so a declaration reads START, then per parameter a PARAM then its PTYPE, then a RETTYPE, then END. A parameter type is the first identifier after the parameter's `:`, and the return type is the first parenthesis-depth-zero identifier once the parameter parentheses close (the identifier after `->`). Types are simple named types this increment. **Increment 5 reads the array type `[T; N]`**, the one nested type the runtime's own data blocks and parser.kel depend on: when a type position opens with `[`, the leading identifier is the element type (emitted as the usual PTYPE or RETTYPE) and the integer literal after the `;` is the length, emitted as an `ASIZE` element (dkind 8) that upgrades the just-emitted type to an array, so the host reconstructs `[T; N]`. The element is a simple named type and the length a literal this increment; an arbitrarily nested element or a const-expression length needs the work-stack walk of the sub-type and stays a later increment, together with the other nested types (a generic `Foo<T>`, a tuple `(A, B)`, an `Option<T>`) — the first place the R3.1 idiom is genuinely exercised in the parser. **Increment 6 recognises the `shared` and `private` `data` block declaration**, the runtime's own state segments (parser.kel's own `toks` and `ps` blocks are two): `<shared\|private> data NAME { field: type, ... }` emits a `DSTART` element (dkind 9; `val = name*4 + visibility`, 0 shared / 1 private) then, per field, the same PARAM/PTYPE(/ASIZE for an array-typed field) elements as a function parameter, then END. The mode machine gains four states (expect `data`, the name, the opening brace, and the field-list scan) and the field list reuses the parameter-scan flags. **Increment 7 adds the `const data` block** (visibility 2), whose fields carry a `= initializer` value: the name and field types are read exactly as a shared or private block, and the initializer is skipped by a depth-aware scan from the `=` to the field-terminating `,` or the block-closing `}`, tracking `()`, `[]`, and `{}` so a nested tuple, array, or struct initializer is consumed whole rather than its commas splitting the field list. Capturing the initializer VALUES, which codegen bakes as constant loads, is a later increment; increment 7 reads the const block's shape only. **Increment 8 adds the bare `use path::name` native import**, the natives contract every host-embedding program depends on: a `USTART` element (dkind 10) opens it and each `::`-separated identifier is a `UPATH` element (dkind 11), the host taking the last as the imported name and the rest as the module path. A `use` has no closing delimiter, so mode 8 ends the declaration when the token after a segment is not `::`, pushing that token back (the cursor steps back one) to open the next declaration, and a `use` running to end-of-input is closed before DONE. The `external` modifier, a `::*` wildcard, and a parenthesised native signature are later increments. **Increment 9 adds the `enum` declaration** `enum Name { Variant, Variant = N, ... }`, the type the stages key their token, node, and opcode codes on now that the runtime and body parser support enum-variant dispatch and the `Enum::Variant() as Word` fold: an `ENUMSTART` element (dkind 12) carries the name, an `EVARIANT` (dkind 13) each variant name, an optional `EDISC` (dkind 14) an explicit `= N` discriminant, and `END` closes the enum. Its five modes are extracted into a `step_enum_decl` helper, the parser's first, because inlining them in the monolithic mode machine pushed its if-else nesting past the reference parser's recursion-depth limit; a helper call resets that depth, the same discipline the body parser adopted for its statement dispatch. The drivers now compile the stage on a wider-stack thread, since the deeper stage overflows the default test-thread stack. Implicit and explicit discriminants are both read; a payload variant, a generic enum, and a discriminant expression are later increments. **The parsed body begins as a separate companion loop `kel/body.kel`** (`tests/selfhost_body.rs`), which reads a function body's tokens and produces the abstract-syntax node forest codegen consumes (`ast.kinds`/`args`/`lhs`/`rhs`), emitting nodes in POSTORDER so the host rebuilds the `lhs`/`rhs` links with one node-index stack. Keeping it separate from parser.kel leaves the declaration parser's brace-skip intact while the body walk grows construct by construct, as codegen itself was built; merging the two into one pass is a later step once the body parser is complete. **Body increment 1** lowers an atomic body — one integer literal (Literal, kind 1, arg = value) or one parameter reference (Local, kind 2, arg = the frame slot resolved by a bounded scan of the host-supplied parameter table) — checked against the reference flattening of the same tail expression. **Body increment 2** adds the binary arithmetic and comparison operators (BinOp, kind 3, arg = the operator code 1 Add … 11 GtEq matching codegen), the first interior nodes that pop children off the host stack. They are parsed by a shunting-yard whose operator-precedence stack lives in the loop's private data; because the loop yields one record per iteration, the shunting-yard's "pop while equal-or-higher precedence" inner loop is spread across iterations by a `resolving` sub-state (one pop per iteration, then the push), and an end-of-body `draining` sub-state flushes the remaining operators. Multiplicative binds tighter than additive tighter than comparison, and every operator is left-associative; precedence and associativity are verified against the reference parser's own tree. **Body increment 3** adds parenthesised grouping: `(` pushes a marker (operator code 15, precedence 0, below every real operator) that no precedence comparison ever pops, and `)` runs a `closing` sub-state that flushes the operators stacked above the marker before discarding it. Because the reference AST records grouping structurally rather than as a node, the reference flattening is unchanged and the produced forest is checked against it. **Body increment 4** adds the unary prefix operators `-` (Neg, node kind 10) and `not` (Not, node kind 6), each holding its single operand in `lhs`. Because they are prefix, an `expect_operand` flag distinguishes a `-` in operand position (negation) from one in operator position (subtraction), and a unary operator is pushed straight onto the operator stack (carrying an internal code 16 or 17 at precedence 4, tighter than multiplicative) rather than settled by precedence, so successive unary operators nest right-associatively; when popped it emits its node kind through an `emit_op` helper and the host pops a single child. **Body increment 5** adds the bitwise operators (`band`/`bor`/`bxor`, BinOp codes 12/13/14) and the short-circuit booleans (`andalso`/`orelse`, their own node kinds 8/9, popping two children), completing the operator-precedence chain to match the reference: `prec_of` is renumbered so unary (9) binds tighter than multiplicative (8) than additive (7) than bitwise (band 6, bxor 5, bor 4) than comparison (3) than andalso (2) than orelse (1), and `opcode_of` and `emit_op` are extended so the short-circuit codes emit their distinct node kinds. The bitwise `band > bxor > bor` and logical `andalso > orelse` sub-orderings match the reference's binding powers, verified against its tree. The eager `and`/`or`/`xor` are omitted because codegen does not yet lower them. **Body increment 6** turns the body from a single expression into a block: zero or more `let name = value;` bindings followed by a tail expression. Each `let` value is an ordinary expression drained at its `;` (its node left on the host stack); the binding claims the next frame slot (`param_count` upward) and joins the scope, so a later value or the tail resolves it as a Local — resolution scans the `let` bindings (a later binding shadowing an earlier one and a parameter) then the parameters. At the `}` the tail is drained and then a LetIn node (kind 5, arg = slot, `lhs` the value, `rhs` the continuation) is emitted for each binding from last to first, folding the tail into the cons-list codegen expects. A `let_phase` state machine reads the binding name and skips an optional `: type` annotation, `drain_reason` distinguishes the `;` drain from the `}` drain, and a `folding` sub-state emits the LetIn chain. **Body increment 7** adds the scalar data-field read `d.f` (DataRead, node kind 11), the operand the stages use most. The loop takes a new field-layout input — parallel `fdata`/`ffield`/`fbase`/`flen` arrays, built from the compiled program's flat data segment — and when an identifier that names a data block is read, a `da_phase` awaits the `.` and then the field name, whose slot is looked up and emitted as a DataRead operand. The harness compiles the program (only when it declares a data block) to obtain the slot names, derives the field table, and resolves a `FieldAccess` in the reference to the same slot; its drivers now run on a wider-stack thread because the host compiler's recursive-descent parse of the increasingly nested stage overflows the default test-thread stack. **Body increment 8** adds the indexed data read `d.f[i]` (IndexRead, node kind 13, arg = base + len*65536, `lhs` the index): after a field, a `da_phase` awaits a `[`, which pushes the field's base/length on a small `idx` stack and an index marker (operator code 20) that blocks precedence across the index sub-expression; the closing `]` runs an `iclosing` sub-state that flushes the sub-expression and emits the IndexRead over the index operand, and the `idx` stack lets a data read nest inside the index (`a.xs[b.i]`). Reaching this depth forced the stage past the reference parser's recursion-depth limit, so `loop main` was refactored into a set of shallow `fn` steps — each returning the record word to yield and mutating the state — mirroring how codegen.kel is structured; a function boundary resets the parser's depth counter, so the stage can keep growing. A non-`[` token after a field is a scalar read whose token is pushed back. **Body increment 9** adds the scalar data-field assignment statement `data.field = value;` (DataFieldAssignIn, node kind 12), the write counterpart to the reads: after a field a `=` opens an assignment whose target slot is recorded, the value is an ordinary expression drained at its `;`, and the statement joins the block. The block's statement list is generalised from `let`-only to a `stmt_kind`/`stmt_arg` pair per statement, so the fold at the `}` emits a LetIn (kind 5) or a DataFieldAssignIn (kind 12) for each statement from last to first, each wrapping the running continuation with its value; a `pending_stmt` flag set at the `=` records which. Indexed assignment `d.f[i] = e;` reorders the value ahead of the index (an IndexStore, kind 15, then IndexAssignIn, kind 14) against source order and is a later increment. **Body increment 10** adds the `if cond { then } else { else }` conditional (If, node kind 4, arg = the condition node, `lhs`/`rhs` the branches; the host pops the three sub-nodes cond/then/else emitted in that order), the first construct with nested branch blocks and so the first genuine recursion in the body walk. An `if`-context stack `if_phase`/`if_sp` tracks each pending conditional's phase (condition, then, awaiting `else`, awaiting the else brace, else), so a nested conditional resolves its `{`, `}`, and `else` against the innermost; the condition and each branch are ordinary expressions drained at the delimiter that ends them (the `{` after the condition and a branch's `}`, via new drain reasons 2/3/4), and the else branch's `}` emits the If. **Body increment 11** generalises an `if` branch from a single expression into a full nested statement block. Each branch's statements accumulate onto the shared statement list from a recorded start (`if_stmt_start`), and its closing `}` folds only that block's statements — from `fold_start` to the statement count, with `fold_cont` selecting the body's DONE, a then branch's phase advance, or an else branch's If — into the branch's cons-list root before restoring the count; the body and both branches now share one `flatten_block`-style path. Two subtleties fell out: local slots come from a monotonic counter that is never reused, so the scope need not be truncated at a block boundary (last-wins resolution over the full binding list matches the reference for every admissible program), and the pending-statement marker became a stack, because a nested statement inside a `let`'s value (`let a = if c { d.x = n; n } else { 0 };`) would otherwise clobber the enclosing statement's kind. **Body increment 12** adds function calls (Call, node kind 7), the body parser's first side-array output. When an identifier names a chunk, a `call_phase` awaits the `(`, which pushes a call context (the callee's chunk index and a running argument count) and a call marker (operator code 21) that blocks precedence across the arguments; each `,` drains the current argument to the marker and counts it (a `cdraining` sub-state), and the closing `)` drains the last argument, counts it when present (the pre-`)` `expect_operand` distinguishes `f()` from `f(a)`), and emits the Call, whose record packs `chunk + count*256`. The host pops the counted argument nodes into a `call_args` side array — reversed to source order — and points the Call node's `lhs`/`rhs` at their start and count, exactly as codegen consumes them; the harness now compiles nothing extra but reads chunk names from the program's function order, drives the last function as the caller, and checks the produced `call_args` against the reference alongside the node forest. **Body increment 13 widens the node-record encoding** from a 4-bit kind (`kind + arg*16`) to a 6-bit kind (`kind + arg*64`), a behaviour-preserving prerequisite so a node kind above 15 (the previous DONE-sentinel ceiling) is representable; the host decode splits `kind = w%64`, `arg = w/64`, the seven `* 16` emission sites become `* 64`, and DONE stays 15, PENDING 0. **Body increment 14 splits slot allocation from scope position** with a monotonic `slot_count` (a frame slot is `param_count + slot_count`), so a binding's slot is independent of its index in the named-binding list and an anonymous slot carrying no name can be claimed. The former scheme took a `let` slot as the scope length, which held only while every slot matched exactly one named binding; the `for..limit` loop breaks that by claiming four unnamed working slots. **Body increment 15 adds the bounded `for v in start..end limit CAP { body }` loop** (ForLimit, node kind 23), the runtime-range-with-static-cap loop the WCET analysis bounds by the literal `CAP`. The loop claims five consecutive frame slots (the variable, then four anonymous working slots for the end, the counter, the cap, and the outcome, the variable first and allocated after the start and before the end), and a `for_emit` sub-state streams the four literal nodes (cap, 0, 1, 2) and five slot records then a build signal, so the host assembles a 12-word `limit_parts` side-array entry (the five slots then the start, end, body, cap, 0, 1, and 2 node indices) and records a ForLimit statement whose entry start is `12 * forlimit_count`. The statement joins the block fold like a `let`, wrapping the running continuation; the host decode pops the seven loop nodes after the build signal, the five slot records into a scratch buffer, and the ForLimit statement its single continuation. This increment handles the non-nested expression-body form. **Body increment 16 generalises a block from a mandatory tail expression to a statement-only block** whose implicit value is Unit (node kind 20, the codegen `PushImmediate(0)`), and folds a `for` body's statements into its body node. A block that closes while an operand is still expected has no tail expression, so at the `}` a Unit node is emitted for the fold to wrap; the `for` body, previously a single expression, now records its statement start at the opening `{` and, at the closing `}`, folds its statements around the body value (a new fold continuation) before the ForLimit emission, exactly as an `if` branch folds. This lands the realistic effectful accumulator loop `for i in start..end limit CAP { acc.f = acc.f + i; }`, whose body is one or more data-field assignments and no tail. **Body increment 17 adds the bare expression statement `expr;`** (ExprStmt, node kind 21), a call or block-form expression evaluated for effect with its value discarded, the write-free statement the stages use to drive a native for its side effect. The challenge is that a `;` no longer implies a `let` or an assignment, so committing the statement must distinguish a bare expression from those two. Each block records a pending-statement baseline, the `pstmt` stack height at the block's entry, pushed onto a `pbase` stack when a branch or a `for` body opens and popped when it folds; at a `;`, a `pstmt` entry above the current block's baseline is the statement's own `let` or assignment (its `=` pushed it) and commits as before, and no such entry means a bare expression that commits as an ExprStmt whose value node is discarded through the fold's continuation link. The baseline is load-bearing under nesting, a bare expression statement inside a `let`'s value block (`let a = if c { g(n); n } else { 0 };`) would otherwise pop and mis-commit the enclosing `let` as an expression statement, a valid-but-wrong forest, so a torture test asserts the inner `g(n);` lowers to an ExprStmt while the outer `let` remains a LetIn. A `yield` composes as an ordinary operand, so it may be a block tail (`yield step()`), a bare expression statement (`yield step();`), or a `let` value (`let x = yield g(a);`). The harness's declaration-keyword finder is hardened to scan at brace depth zero, since a body may now itself contain a `yield` token and a last-occurrence scan would otherwise latch onto it rather than the function's own keyword. ## Surface-language and runtime prerequisites

Per the design's "Required surface-language features", most of the surface is already
sufficient as of V0.2.0. The items that still need *work*, not surface changes, are tied
to the stage that needs them rather than to a release number:

- **For the lexer.** The `compiler::intern_bytes` / `text_from_bytes` / `text_concat`
  natives, and the byte-array source-input path (already exercised by increment 1). No
  surface-language extension is required (R3.3).
- **For the parser.** The work-stack idiom (R3.1), so recursive grammar is walked
  without `fn`/`yield` recursion. Whether to relax the recursion rule for the compiler
  instead of using explicit stacks is the one open surface question; the default is
  explicit stacks.
- **For codegen.** The compiler-in-Keleusma is written in the **explicitly-annotated
  subset** so that the compiler checking itself does not stress its own Hindley-Milner
  inference. Inference is bounded to per-declaration scope, and the monomorphization
  specialization table is bounded persistent `data`-block state (R3.4, R5.3). None of
  this requires a surface change; it is a discipline on how the compiler's own source is
  written.

## What V0.3.0 does not do

V0.3.0 does not retire the Rust-hosted compiler. The dual-compiler period is
intentional. The Rust-hosted compiler stays the reference implementation and the
equivalence oracle, and the self-hosted compiler is the proof that the language admits
its own toolchain. Native code generation (Keleusma to LLVM to native) is the separate
V0.4.0 effort and depends on this milestone landing first; see
[`docs/roadmap/V0_4_0_NATIVE_CODEGEN.md`](../docs/roadmap/V0_4_0_NATIVE_CODEGEN.md).
